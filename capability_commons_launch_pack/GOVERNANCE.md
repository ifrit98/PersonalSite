# Governance

## Purpose

Capability Commons exists to build durable public capability through open practical knowledge.

The project publishes learning paths, modules, worksheets, reference sheets, teach-forward packets, and the technical infrastructure required to maintain them responsibly over time.

## Founder and steward

Capability Commons was founded by **Jason St George**, who serves as the initial steward of the project, editor of the canonical public surface, and maintainer of official releases.

## Governance model

Capability Commons uses a stewarded open-project model.

This means:

- the codebase and content may be openly developed
- contributors are welcome
- review is visible
- evidence and revision history are preserved
- official releases and canonical editorial decisions remain with project maintainers

## Maintainer roles

### Steward

Sets direction, approves maintainers, resolves disputes, and controls official releases.

### Maintainers

Review pull requests, curate content, approve revisions, and keep the platform operational.

### Contributors

Submit code, content, corrections, evidence, field reports, and local adaptations.

## Decision principles

When conflicts arise, the project prefers:

1. clarity over volume
2. evidence over assertion
3. usability over ornamental complexity
4. revision over rigidity
5. public capability over prestige signaling

## Content policy

Canonical public content should be:

- plain-language accessible
- practically useful
- evidence-linked where appropriate
- explicit about uncertainty
- clear about boundaries, risks, and stop conditions
- structured so it can be taught forward

## Official and unofficial versions

Forks, adaptations, and downstream deployments are permitted under the project licenses.

Only releases published by the official project maintainers may present themselves as the canonical or official version of Capability Commons.

## Amendments

This governance document may be updated by the steward and maintainers as the project matures.
