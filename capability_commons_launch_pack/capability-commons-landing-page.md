# Capability Commons

Open practical knowledge for resilience, systems literacy, and teach-forward public competence.

Capability Commons is an open knowledge system that maps concepts to skills, skills to projects, and projects to local deployment. Its aim is simple: recover practical knowledge, make it legible, and help people teach it forward.

## What it is

Most people do not need more information. They need clearer paths from understanding to action.

Capability Commons is built around reproducible capability. Each module is designed to help someone understand a principle, perform a task, produce a durable artifact, and explain it clearly to someone else.

This is not a content feed. It is a public capability system.

## How it works

Capability Commons organizes knowledge as a graph:

- concepts explain what something is
- skills define what someone can do
- projects turn understanding into useful artifacts
- modules sequence the work into learnable paths
- teach-forward packets help each learner become a transmitter

Under the surface, the Commons is powered by a versioned evidence and retrieval layer so guidance can be reviewed, updated, localized, and improved over time.

## Why now

Practical competence has become fragmented, gated, outsourced, or trapped behind institutions, jargon, and disappearing local memory.

The point of Capability Commons is not nostalgia. It is public capacity.

If useful knowledge can be recovered, clarified, and taught forward, more people can become less helpless in the face of fragile systems and more useful to one another.
