Yes — you should have your own website.

For you, it should not be a generic “portfolio site.” It should be your **canonical public proof layer**: the place where your engineering, research, writing, and intellectual work become legible as one coherent body of work rather than a collection of interesting fragments. Your updated resume already gives you the spine for that: **Protocol & Infrastructure Lead**, privacy-preserving/adversarial systems, distributed systems + proofs + real-time ML, selected wins across DSN, Navy work, AfterFiat, and peer-reviewed research, plus the musician/educator dimension. 

## The core strategic move

Do **not** treat this as “resume site + links.”

Treat it as a **master brand with endorsed satellites**.

Your name should be the durable root layer. Company names change. publications evolve. projects fork. Your name is the stable namespace that ties together:

* engineering/operator credibility
* research seriousness
* long-form writing / worldview
* artistic/formal work in music and mathematics

That fits your actual public profile better than forcing everything under one company or one publication.

## Recommended brand architecture

Use an **endorsed ecosystem**:

**Master brand:**
**Jason St George**

**Endorsed properties:**

* **Eschatology Report** — publication / worldview / essays
* **AfterFiat** — thesis, paper, diagrams, references
* **AlchemicalAI** — company / studio / consulting / products
* **Music geometric / mathematical framework** — research project, then separate site later if it earns one
* **Published research** — housed under your personal site, with dedicated pages for each paper
* **Engineering / systems work** — housed under your personal site, with selected case studies and sanitized summaries

**Private or selectively surfaced:**

* **DSN**
* deeper classified / sensitive work

The DSN material is too strong to disappear completely, but it also does not need to be a front-page brand object. Public version: one concise, high-level mention. Private version: a better-written dossier or case study PDF you send selectively.

## The right public positioning

Your public site should unify everything under one sentence:

**“I build and write about systems for truth, coordination, and value under adversarial conditions.”**

That line is broad enough to hold:

* defense / real-time ML
* decentralized systems and proofs
* AfterFiat / trust infrastructure
* Eschatology Report / synthetic media, social breakdown, civilizational framing
* music geometry / formal structure and invariants

That is the thread. Not “I do many things.”
The thread is: **verification, structure, signal, and trust under difficult conditions**.

## What the site should optimize for

You said the goals are to **legitimize yourself and monetize your skills / writing / research**.

That means the site needs to do four jobs quickly:

1. **Identify** — who you are in one sentence
2. **Explain** — what domains you work across
3. **Prove** — why you are credible
4. **Route** — where the visitor should go next

That routing matters because you actually have multiple audiences:

* recruiters / hiring managers
* consulting clients
* readers / subscribers
* research collaborators
* people arriving from one property who need to discover the others

## Best aesthetic for your goals

You should not go full cyberpunk, full startup, or full occult-editorial.

You need a hybrid that feels like:

**institutional credibility + editorial seriousness + controlled symbolic depth**

The balance I would use is:

* **70% institutional**
* **20% editorial**
* **10% mythic / symbolic**

That means:

### What it should feel like

Think:
**Bell Labs monograph + serious modern research lab + premium essay publication**

### What it should not feel like

Avoid:

* neon crypto aesthetics
* “hacker movie” black/red visual language
* too much alchemical/esoteric symbolism on the homepage
* cluttered portfolio-grid energy
* startup-bro SaaS site tropes

### Recommended visual language

* **Default light theme** or warm-paper theme for trust and legibility
* Optional dark mode, but not the default
* Serif headlines + clean sans-serif body
* restrained mono for technical details
* subtle geometric diagrams, thin-line network forms, topological or musical motifs as accents
* one strong black-and-white portrait or restrained studio photo

### Palette

Something like:

* deep navy / graphite
* warm off-white / bone
* muted gold or brass accent
* maybe one desaturated forest or oxblood accent for depth

That palette lets you feel serious, learned, and slightly distinctive without looking theatrical.

## Technical setup

Because you are technical, keep the stack clean and durable.

### Good approach

* static-first site
* markdown / MDX content
* shared design system
* fast load times
* strong typography
* minimal plugins
* privacy-conscious analytics

### Stack direction

* **Astro** if you want a publication-first, content-heavy site
* **Next.js** if you want more app-like interactivity and a shared component layer across properties

### Recommended basics

* one monorepo
* shared theme tokens/components for the personal site and future sub-sites
* Plausible or self-hosted analytics
* good metadata / OG images / structured data
* RSS
* sitemap
* canonical URLs
* contact form with a serious intake flow
* downloadable PDFs for resume / CV / selected papers

## The biggest mistake to avoid

Do not make the homepage a list of all your interests.

That would make the breadth look unfocused.

Instead, make the homepage about the **one underlying operating question** you keep returning to:
how do we build trustworthy systems — technical, social, informational, aesthetic — under adversarial and synthetic conditions?

That is your brand.
